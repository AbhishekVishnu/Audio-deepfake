§ 01 — Editorial

Tell a human voice
from a machine's impersonation.

TrueVoice is a forensic listening console. Drop in a recording, paste a link, or speak into the microphone. The bureau analyses it with a self-supervised Wav2Vec2 detector, returning a verdict, a confidence score, a per-chunk timeline of suspicion, a mel-spectrogram of what the model heard, and a robustness panel that puts the verdict to the test.

§ 07 — Method

How the bureau decides.

  1. i.

    Self-supervised features

    A pretrained Wav2Vec2 transformer encodes the raw waveform. No hand-crafted MFCCs. The encoder has heard millions of hours of human speech and is sensitive to the small inconsistencies that synthesis leaves behind.

  2. ii.

    Chunked & ensembled

    Audio is split into overlapping five-second windows. Each window is scored independently; per-chunk probabilities are averaged for a stable verdict, optionally across multiple models.

  3. iii.

    Explained & tested

    You see exactly where the model is suspicious, what its mel-spectrogram looks like, and how the verdict moves under noise, codec compression, pitch and reverb — the everyday corruptions that a real-world clip will face.
